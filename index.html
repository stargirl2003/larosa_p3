Who's That Bird?

An Interactive Art Piece

Description

A fun drawing exercise where participants create their own little flock of birds together!

Materials:
- Multiple Sheets of Paper
-Markers
Number of Participants:
- 2 or 4
Time to Complete:
8 -10 minutes

Instructions

  1. Each person picks their own color
  2. Each person will then get 3 pieces of paper; one will get drawn on and the others will be used to cover up parts of the drawing
  3. The number of birds that will be made for the flock corresponds to the amount of people participating i.e. if there are 3 people 3 birds will be made
  4. Each drawing will be started with the head of the bird and each round you will make your way down the rest of the bird; the order goes head, wings, body, and legs
  5. Set a 2 minute timer and start off drawing the first part of the bird and make sure that everyone else can't see what you are drawing. Once the timer is up, cover up the part you drew leaving only a small part uncovered, switch birds and set another 2 minute timer and start drawing the next step of the bird Repeat this process.
  6. Put each bird next to each other and take off the piece of paper covering the drawing to reveal your new flock of birds that you all created together
  7. Bonus step: take a moment and name each of your birds and the flock itself
